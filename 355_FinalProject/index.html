Canada's Inflation Rate Hits A Fresh 18-year High in 2021

In September 2021, Canada's annual inflation rate climbed to 4.4%, marking its highest level since February 2003, up from 4.1% in August. The surge in inflation was driven by significant price hikes in key sectors, including transport, housing, and food. As everyday costs rise, Canadians are feeling the impact on their wallets, raising the important question: Are wages keeping up with the increasing cost of living?
