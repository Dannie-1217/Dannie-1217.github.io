Wage Growth by Industry

We aim to create a bar chart that visualizes wage growth across different years by comparing areas rather than stacking the bars segment by segment. In other words, instead of bulding each year's data upon the previous year's, we want to start all yearly wages from the x-axis so we can easily see the growth. Besides, we will also sort the bar chart according to the wage as well.

Weighted CPI Growth vs Wage Growth

For this visualization, we plan to do a trivariate bubble chart with scaling bubble size and two colors. This chart will integrate wage growth and CPI growth of different occupations across years. The bubble size will refer to the wage growth rate and CPI growth rate with different two colors. When the user hovers on the bubbles, they turn red when CPI growth exceeds wage growth in a category (negative impact), or green when wage growth surpasses CPI (positive impact), with percentage labels (e.g., “+3%” or “-3%”) for clarity. This chart can offer an at-a-glance view of the comparison between wage growth and CPI growth, enabling the user to immediately see whether their wage growth is sufficient or lacking.
We are still figuring out how to do this chart. This is just a draft.
